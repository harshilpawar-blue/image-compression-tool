JPG vs. PNG: Which Format Should You Use?

When you save an image, you usually have two main choices: JPG or PNG. Choosing the wrong one can make your website slow or your pictures blurry.

Here is the simple rule to help you decide.

When to use JPG

Best for: Photographs, portraits, and images with millions of colors.

JPG is designed to compress photos efficiently. It keeps the file size small so pages load fast. However, it does not support transparent backgrounds.

When to use PNG

Best for: Logos, screenshots, text graphics, and transparent backgrounds.

PNG keeps lines and text perfectly sharp. It also allows for transparency (invisible backgrounds). The trade-off is that PNG files are often larger than JPGs.

Need to switch formats?

If you have a PNG photo that is too big, convert it to JPG to save space. If you have a graphic that needs to be sharp, convert it to PNG.
