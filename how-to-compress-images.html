How to Make Images Smaller for the Web

Do you have photos you want to put on a website or send in an email? Sometimes, these photos are too big. They take a long time to send, and they make websites load slowly.

We can fix this by "compressing" them. Think of it like squeezing a sponge. The sponge is still a sponge, but it takes up less space.

What does "Compress" mean?
It simply means making the file size smaller without changing how the picture looks to the human eye.

Why should I compress images?

  • Faster Websites: If you own a website, small images make it load very fast. Visitors like fast websites.
  • Save Space: Compressed images take up less room on your computer or phone.
  • Easier Sharing: It is much faster to email or text a small photo than a large one.

How to do it (Step-by-Step)

You do not need expensive software. You can do this right here, for free.

  1. Open the Tool: Go to our Image Compressor tool.
  2. Select Your Photos: Click the big blue button that says "Select Images". Pick the photos from your computer.
  3. Wait a Moment: The tool will automatically squeeze the file size down.
  4. Download: Click "Download" to save your new, smaller photos.

That is all there is to it. Your photos will look the same, but they will be much lighter and easier to handle.
