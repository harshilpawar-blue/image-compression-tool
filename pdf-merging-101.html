A Simple Guide to Merging PDF Files

Do you have several PDF files scattered on your computer? Maybe you have one file for a cover letter, another for your resume, and a third for your portfolio.

Sending three separate emails is messy. It is much better to combine them into one single document. This process is called "Merging".

What does "Merge" mean?
Merging means taking two or more separate files and stitching them together, one after the other, to create a single new file.

Why combine your PDF files?

  • Get Organized: Keep related documents together so you do not lose them.
  • Look Professional: Sending one clean file looks much better than sending five small attachments.
  • Easier Printing: You only have to click "Print" once instead of opening every single file separately.

How to do it (Step-by-Step)

You can do this right now using our free tool. It works on your phone or computer.

  1. Open the Tool: Go to our PDF Merger tool.
  2. Select Files: Click "Select PDF Files" and pick all the documents you want to combine.
  3. Arrange Them: Use the Up and Down arrows to put them in the correct order (e.g., Cover Letter first, Resume second).
  4. Merge: Click the "Merge PDF" button. The tool will instantly create your new document.

That is it! You now have one organized file ready to share.
