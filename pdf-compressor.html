Optimize PDFs for Email and Web

PDF files often contain redundant data, uncompressed images, and hidden metadata that bloat their file size. The Image Toolkit provides two specialized engines to fix this.

1. Lossless Compression (Standard)

This mode reorganizes the PDF's internal code structure. It removes unused objects and optimizes fonts without touching the visual content. Use this for legal documents, CVs, and invoices where clarity is paramount.

2. Strong Compression (Rasterization)

Sometimes you just need the file to be small (e.g., under 2MB for an upload portal). Strong mode converts each page into a highly optimized image and rebuilds the PDF. This yields massive size reductions but makes text non-searchable.

Why compress PDFs?

  • Faster Uploads: Small files upload in seconds, even on slow connections.
  • Email Limits: Most email providers block attachments larger than 25MB. Compression solves this.
  • Storage: Save space on your cloud drive or hard disk.
